Centipede

Description

In Puerto Rico, people commonly call the large centipede an “alacrán.” It is an elongated arthropod with many legs, a segmented body, and mostly nocturnal behavior. It usually hides in humid, dark, and protected areas.

Risks

  • Painful bite.
  • Localized swelling.
  • Intense temporary discomfort.
  • Greater risk for children and sensitive individuals.

Why do they appear?

  • Humid environments or leaks.
  • The presence of other insects, which serve as food.
  • Cracks, gaps, and unsealed entry points.
  • Accumulated debris, wood, or vegetation near the structure.

Prevention

  • Reduce humidity and repair leaks.
  • Seal cracks and entry points.
  • Remove harborage areas such as stacked wood or dry leaves.
  • Control other pests that may serve as a food source.
