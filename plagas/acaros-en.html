Mites

What are they?

Mites are microscopic arthropods related to spiders and ticks. Indoors, the most common are household dust mites, which feed on skin flakes and thrive in warm, humid environments.

Risks

  • They can trigger respiratory allergies such as sneezing, congestion, and itching.
  • They are associated with asthma and worsening symptoms in sensitive individuals.
  • In some cases, they may cause irritation or skin reactions.

Where are they found?

  • Mattresses and pillows.
  • Upholstery such as sofas, chairs, and curtains.
  • Carpets and dusty areas.
  • Humid places with poor ventilation.

Prevention

  • Reduce humidity, ideally with ventilation and/or a dehumidifier.
  • Wash bedding frequently and use mite-proof covers.
  • Vacuum regularly, preferably with a HEPA filter.
  • Avoid dust buildup and keep rooms well ventilated.
