Mosquito Aedes aegypti

Description

The Aedes aegypti is a common mosquito in Puerto Rico and is often associated with residential areas. It breeds in standing water, even in very small containers such as bottle caps, tires, plant pots, buckets, and any container that holds water for several days.

Why is it important?

This mosquito represents a public health concern because it is associated with mosquito-borne diseases. For that reason, control and prevention are essential, especially by eliminating breeding sites around the home.

Signs and habits

  • Greater activity near homes and yards.
  • It breeds in containers with standing water, including very small ones.
  • It may bite mainly during the day, depending on environmental conditions.
  • It rests in shaded and protected areas such as vegetation, under furniture, and in humid corners.

Prevention (most effective)

  • Eliminate standing water every week from pots, buckets, tires, pet bowls, and containers.
  • Keep tanks and cisterns properly covered.
  • Clean gutters, drains, and outdoor areas where water accumulates.
  • Turn over or store containers that can fill with rainwater.
  • Use screens and reduce entry points into the home.
