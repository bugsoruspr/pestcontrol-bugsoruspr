House Mouse (Mus musculus)

Description

In Puerto Rico, the term “arriero” or “jarriero” is commonly used to refer to the house mouse. It is a small rodent that adapts easily to urban environments and can enter through very small openings in search of food and shelter.

Characteristics

  • Small size (7–10 cm not including the tail).
  • Gray or light brown color.
  • High reproductive capacity.
  • Mostly active at night.

Risks

  • Contamination of food and surfaces.
  • Damage to electrical wiring and structures.
  • Risk of rodent-related diseases.
  • Fast infestations if not controlled early.

Signs of infestation

  • Small dark droppings.
  • Noise in walls or ceilings.
  • Chewed food or damaged packaging.
  • Strong odor in enclosed areas.

Prevention

  • Seal cracks and openings smaller than 1/4 inch.
  • Store food in airtight containers.
  • Keep trash tightly closed.
  • Reduce clutter and harborage areas.
