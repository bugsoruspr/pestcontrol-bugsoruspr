Fire Ant (Solenopsis spp.)

Description

The fire ant is known for its aggressive behavior when the nest is disturbed. In yards and green areas, it can form visible loose-soil mounds and may sometimes enter structures in search of food or shelter.

Risks

  • Painful stings with a burning sensation.
  • Strong reactions in sensitive or allergic individuals.
  • Risk for children and pets playing in yards or gardens.

Signs of infestation

  • Mounds or loose soil buildup in lawns or gardens.
  • Active ants in the yard, on sidewalks, or around planters.
  • Frequent stings in outdoor areas.

Prevention

  • Avoid debris accumulation and excess moisture near the home.
  • Keep lawns and yards clean and well drained.
  • Seal access points into the structure, including cracks and poorly sealed doors.

Professional control

Effective control requires a clear strategy: treatment of the nest or complete colony and environmental management to reduce the risk of reinfestation.
