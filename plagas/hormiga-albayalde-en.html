Albayalde Ant

Description

In Puerto Rico, the name “hormiga albayalde” is used for a small ant with orange or reddish tones that may invade indoor and outdoor areas. It can establish nests in soil, cracks, planters, or protected areas near structures.

Risks and problems

  • It infests kitchens and food-related areas.
  • It may appear in large numbers over short periods.
  • If the nest is close to the structure, it can return even after visible trails are cleaned.

Why do they appear?

  • Available food such as grease, sugar, and crumbs.
  • Moisture and shelter in cracks, planters, wood, and debris.
  • Unsealed access points around doors and windows.

Prevention

  • Seal cracks and entry points.
  • Keep the exterior free of leaves and debris buildup.
  • Store food in airtight containers.
  • Reduce sources of moisture near the structure.
