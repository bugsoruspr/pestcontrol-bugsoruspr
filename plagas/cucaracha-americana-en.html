American Cockroach (Periplaneta americana)

Description

The American cockroach is one of the largest cockroach species found in Puerto Rico. It prefers humid environments such as basements, sewers, drains, and outdoor areas. It may enter a building in search of food, water, or shelter.

Characteristics

  • Large size (up to 4 cm).
  • Reddish-brown color.
  • Limited flying ability.
  • Mostly active at night.

Risks

  • Contamination of surfaces and food.
  • Mechanical transport of bacteria and pathogens.
  • Unpleasant odor in severe infestations.
  • A nuisance and sanitation concern in commercial and residential settings.

Signs of infestation

  • Sightings in humid areas or near drains.
  • Droppings that look like small dark grains.
  • A strong odor in large infestations.

Prevention

  • Reduce moisture and repair leaks.
  • Keep drains clean.
  • Seal cracks and entry points.
  • Remove accumulated trash and organic debris.
